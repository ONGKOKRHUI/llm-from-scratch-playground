The Future of Systems Programming: Rust vs C++
By Jonathan Doe | October 12, 2023

For decades, C++ has held the crown for systems programming. It offers unparalleled control over memory and hardware. However, the rise of Rust has introduced a serious contender to the throne.

Rust’s primary selling point is memory safety without garbage collection. Through its ownership model, it prevents data races at compile time—a class of bugs that has plagued C++ developers for years.

"I switched to Rust three years ago and haven't looked back," says Sarah Jenkins, a Senior Backend Engineer. "The compiler is strict, but it teaches you to write better code."

Thread: Why are my tomato leaves turning yellow?
Started by: GreenThumb99

GreenThumb99:
Hi everyone, I planted my tomatoes about 6 weeks ago. They were doing great, but suddenly the bottom leaves are turning yellow and spotting. I water them every day. Is it a fungus?

Replies:

GardenerDave:
It sounds like Early Blight, but it could also be overwatering. Tomatoes don't like "wet feet." Let the soil dry out a bit between waterings.

PlantLady:
+1 on the Early Blight. Try trimming the affected leaves and applying a copper fungicide. Also, make sure you aren't splashing water on the leaves when you irrigate.
